Support & Help

Get help with Warranty Manager. Find answers to common questions or contact us for assistance.

Frequently Asked Questions

How do I install Warranty Manager?

From Chrome Web Store (Recommended):

  1. Visit the Chrome Web Store listing
  2. Click "Add to Chrome"
  3. Grant notification permission when prompted

Developer Installation:

  1. Clone or download the repository
  2. Open Chrome and navigate to chrome://extensions/
  3. Enable "Developer mode" (toggle in top right)
  4. Click "Load unpacked"
  5. Select the extension folder

How do I add a purchase?

  1. Click the extension icon to open the popup
  2. Click "+ Add Purchase"
  3. Fill in all required fields:
    • Product Name (max 200 characters)
    • Purchase Price (0 to 999,999,999)
    • Purchase Date (cannot be in the future)
    • Store/Retailer (max 200 characters)
    • Warranty Period (1 to 36,500 days)
    • Receipt Photo (optional, max 5MB)
  4. Click "Save" to add the purchase

How do notifications work?

Warranty Manager sends browser notifications when warranties are expiring soon. The extension checks daily for expiring warranties and sends notifications based on your stored data.

To test notifications, click "üîî Test Notifications" in the extension popup. You can disable notifications at any time through Chrome's notification settings.

Where is my data stored?

All your data is stored locally on your device using IndexedDB (a browser database). No data is transmitted to external servers. Your data never leaves your device.

To export your data, use the "Export" feature in the extension to download your data as JSON or CSV.

How do I export my data?

  1. Open the extension popup
  2. Click the "Export" button
  3. Choose JSON or CSV format
  4. Your data will be downloaded to your device

How do I delete my data?

You can delete individual purchases from within the extension, or delete all data by:

  1. Uninstalling the extension, or
  2. Clearing your browser's IndexedDB data through Chrome settings

Is my data private?

Yes! Warranty Manager is designed with privacy as a top priority:

  • All data is stored locally on your device
  • No data is transmitted to external servers
  • No tracking or analytics are performed
  • No third-party services are used

See our Privacy Policy for more details.

What permissions does the extension need?

Warranty Manager requires minimal permissions:

  • Notifications: To send alerts when warranties are expiring
  • Alarms: To schedule daily checks for expiring warranties

All processing happens locally on your device.

Can I use this extension offline?

Yes! Warranty Manager operates entirely offline. Once installed, you can use all features without an internet connection. The extension only needs internet access for initial installation from the Chrome Web Store.
